## Recommendation Rules Basis

| Rule | Scientific Basis | Citation Status |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Low risk → all commodities suitable | Standard agricultural practice; absence of stress conditions permits full crop range | General agronomic principle |
| Medium risk → exclude very low tolerance crops | Waterlogging-sensitive species (melon, watermelon) suffer irreversible root damage within hours of saturation | [Pending] – Crop waterlogging tolerance thresholds |
| High risk → only very high/high tolerance crops | During active flood conditions, only species adapted to saturated environments can survive without total loss | [Pending] – Flood-adapted crop selection for disaster resilience |
| Raised beds for medium-tolerance crops at medium risk | Elevation of root zone above water table provides 20–30 cm buffer against brief inundation | [Pending] – Raised bed agriculture in flood-prone areas |
| Delay planting at high risk | Planting during active flooding wastes seed, labour, and resources with near-certain crop failure | General agronomic principle |

## Mitigation Rules Basis

| Action | Scientific/Practical Basis | Citation Status |
|--------|---------------------------|-----------------|
| Drainage inspection (routine) | Preventive maintenance ensures drainage capacity when needed | Standard farm management practice |
| Clear drainage channels | Blocked channels cause localised flooding even during moderate rainfall | [Pending] – Farm drainage infrastructure management |
| Raised bed preparation | Elevates root zone 20–30 cm above field level; effective for waterlogging prevention up to medium flooding | [Pending] – Raised bed cultivation in tropical lowlands |
| Mulching for erosion prevention | Organic mulch reduces raindrop impact, maintains soil structure, reduces surface runoff | [Pending] – Mulching effects on erosion and soil conservation |
| Emergency harvest | Salvaging mature crops before flood damage reduces economic loss | Standard disaster response practice |
| Flood barriers (sandbags) | Physical diversion of floodwater from cultivation areas | Standard disaster mitigation practice |
| Seedling protection (elevation) | Nursery stock represents concentrated future value; elevation protects investment | Standard nursery management |
| Post-flood recovery preparation | Pre-positioned materials reduce recovery time; faster replanting reduces income gap | [Pending] – Post-flood agricultural recovery strategies |
